Privacy Policy for California Residents

In this part we discuss the privacy requirements with respect to personal information, as laid down in the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). If you are a consumer with disabilities, please contact us at ccpa@nationaljusticebureau.com for further assistance.

Rights of California Residents for the Protection of their Personal Information

As regards the protection of personal information collected by businesses, residents of California enjoy certain special rights. Subject to certain limitations and exceptions, California residents have the following rights:

  • the right to know the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell, our purposes for collecting or selling personal information, the sources from which we collect personal information, the categories of your personal information that we have either sold or disclosed for a business purpose, and the categories of third parties with whom we have shared personal information.
  • the right to opt out of our sale(s) of personal information. However, please note that if you opt out of certain types of sales, we will not be able to provide you with the services relying on such sales.
  • the right not to be treated in a discriminatory manner in the exercise of the privacy rights laid down in the CCPA.
  • the right to request that we delete the personal information collected about California resident.

Please contact us by email at ccpa@nationaljusticebureau.com or online at CCPA Request Webform, if you wish to exercise any of the special rights described above.
